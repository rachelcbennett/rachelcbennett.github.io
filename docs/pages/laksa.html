Laksa (Curry Noodles)

Posted by Rachel on February 5, 2021



Laksa was a favorite dish of mine when I lived in Malaysia! I have always loved curry and combining curry and soup was a winning combo in my mind. Here's my recipe for a Curry Laksa Soup, although there are several variations on this classic Malaysian recipe!

I am using onion for the soup base, but feel free to substitute it out for something else if onions aren't your thing. I have made this recipe using a cucumber base, and even a potato base! It's always delicious!

Laksa Recipe

By Rach's Recipes

Ingredients:

  • 1/2 Onion (Or use a different base like cucumber or potato)
  • 3 Tbsps Curry Powder
  • 1 Tsp Turmeric
  • 2 inches of Lemongrass
  • 1 inch of fresh Ginger
  • 2 cloves of Garlic
  • 1 Tsp Salt

  • 3-4 chicken thighs
  • 1 12 oz Can Coconut Milk
  • Rice Noodles (Chao Ching brand is great)

Optional Toppings/Sauces

  • Hoisin Sauce
  • Soy Sauce
  • Thai Basil
  • Bean Sprouts
  • Fresh diced Tomatoes
  • Canned mandarin oranges
  • Diced hardboiled egg, or a fried egg

Instructions:

  1. Boil water in a large pot. This will be for the noodles.

  2. Heat oil in a large skillet. When oil is sufficiently hot, throw diced chicken in. Wait for it to sear, then mix and add salt or any other seasonings desired.

  3. While chicken cooks, combine in blender onion, lemongrass, ginger, garlic, salt, curry powder and turmeric. Blend until the mixture is smooth.

  4. Remove cooked chicken from skillet

  5. Add onion base to the skillet and fry for 2-3 minutes, stirring occasionally.

  6. Add the can of coconut milk, and stir to combine. When it's heated through, add the chicken back in.

  7. Place the noodles (half the package) in a large mixing bowl. Add the boiling water to cook it and let it sit.

To Serve:

In each bowl, place some of the cooked noodles. Ladle the curry soup over the noodles, then add toppings as desired. Yum!
