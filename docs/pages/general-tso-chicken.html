General Tso's Chicken

Posted by Rachel on March 16, 2021



I don't know who General Tso is, and I don't even think this dish is really Chinese... But it's DELICIOUS and so I'm going to share it with you! This is quickly becoming my husband's favorite dish. It's pretty easy, once you get the hang of breading the chicken, etc. For this recipe, we'll be BAKING the chicken instead of deep frying, so it's just that much healthier!

Some notes on Ingredients/Methods:

  • If you've been reading my other recipes, it probably won't surprise you that I'm using chicken thighs instead of chicken breasts. #TeamChickenThigh (please don't actually use that hashtag...)

  • Panko bread crumbs are the best! They're so easy to use! I get them at the Chinese Market, but you should be able to find them most places.

  • I am baking the chicken rather than frying it to make it a little healthier. You can choose to fry it if you like, but the panko really makes a nice texture and I don't even miss the frying.





How to make General Tso's Chicken

Getting Started

Preheat the oven to 400 degrees Fahrenheit.

Line a large baking sheet with tin foil and then spray with cooking spray (I usually use coconut oil)

Dice your chicken thighs into bite sized pieces, and get rid of any excess water with a paper towel.

Put the flour, panko crumbs, and 3 eggs into three separate bowls. Beat the eggs thoroughly and add a pinch of salt.

Breading the Chicken

There are a couple different ways you can do this. I personally use a pair of tongs to grab a piece of chicken, dunk it in the flour, then the egg, and then the breadcrumbs. I think it's a lot faster, and you avoid breading your fingers (usually it gets to the point where I'm like, should I fry these too?) But it's up to you! This will take some time but the tongs speed it up and you'll get faster!

Once you finish, the oven should be ready, so pop them in! Set a timer for 20 minutes.


Making the Sauce

In the meantime, chop up 2 cloves of garlic.

In a small bowl, combine 3/4 cup of Hoisin Sauce and 1/4 cup of Soy Sauce (I usually use lite soy sauce, less sodium). Add a squirt of Sriracha sauce if you have it, but it's not necessary. Whisk together.

Heat a large pan on the stove on medium heat. When the pan is hot, add coconut oil/butter/oil. When the oil is heated, add the garlic and 1 tsp of chili flakes, stirring thoroughly to avoid letting the garlic burn. Once it is nice and yummy smelling, add your sauce! Turn the heat down and let it simmer a bit.

Add the chicken back in, and stir to combine. Add the can of coconut milk, stirring well and turning the heat down to let it simmer. Add the potatoes.


Add the Chicken

Check the chicken. If it's just beginning to get a beautiful brown color on the top, it should be ready. But you can also check it with a meat thermometer that its internal temperature is at or above 165 degrees.

Remove chicken from the oven, and use a spatula or similar tool to remove chicken from the foil. If you oiled it thoroughly it shouldn't stick very much.

Turn the heat to low. Dump the chicken in your pot with the sauce, and quickly stir it to combine. The panko will quickly start to absorb the sauce, so don't dilly dally.

If after stirring to combine, there are still pieces with little sauce, go ahead and just add a bit more hoisin sauce into the pan and stir thoroughly, and a bit more soy sauce. It's hard to predict how much sauce you'll need because of the breading, but don't fret too much, it always works out.

Ta-Da!

That's pretty much it! Dish up the chicken, and add a ton of sesame seeds if you have them for a beautiful look. Serve with rice. I always put some extra chili flakes on the table because some people like it extra spicy!







General Tso's Chicken

By Rach's Recipes

Ingredients:

    For the Breaded Chicken
  • 3-4 Boneless Skinless Chicken Thighs
  • 2 Cups of Panko Bread Crumbs (or other bread crumbs)
  • 3 Eggs
  • Pinch of salt
  • 1-2 Cups of Flour

    • For the Sauce
  • 2-3 Cloves of Garlic, minced
  • 1 Tsp of Chili Flakes (or to taste)
  • 3/4 Cup Hoisin Sauce
  • 1/4 Cup Lite Soy Sauce
  • One squirt of Sriracha Sauce (optional)
  • Sesame Seeds (for garnish, optional)

Instructions:

  1. Clean, peel, and dice potatoes.

  2. Boil water. When water is boiling, toss potatoes in.

  3. Chop chicken into bite sized pieces. Season with soysauce and salt (optional). Chop onions, if desired.

  4. Heat oil in a large skillet. When oil is sufficiently hot, throw chicken in. Don't stir chicken until they've had a chance to sear (1-2 minutes) then stir and add salt, to taste.

  5. When chicken is cooked, remove and place in a separate bowl.

  6. Add a bit more coconut (or vegetable) oil to the pan, then add the carrot matchsticks and onions.

  7. Add the Mae Ploy Panang curry paste.

  8. Stir and let cook until the spices are fragrant and the carrots are soft, then add the chicken back in.

  9. Add the can of coconut milk and stir to combine. Turn heat to low.

  10. Check to see if potatoes are soft. Add potatoes to the curry

  11. Taste the curry, if it is too spicy, add a bit more coconut milk (or almond, regular, soy, etc) to cool it down. You can also add more salt, or even more curry paste at this point if you like it spicier.

  12. Serve with warm rice and garnish with cilantro.
