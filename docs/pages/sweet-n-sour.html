Sweet and Sour Chicken

Posted by Rachel on February 8, 2021



This Sweet and Sour Chicken is such a quick and easy recipe! I make it on weeknights when I want a treat but I don't have much time.


How to make Sweet 'n Sour Chicken

Getting Started

Place your chicken nuggets (or chicken patties, or chicken tenders) onto a baking sheet and bake according to package instructions. If you're using chicken patties or chicken tenders, you might want to get this started a bit earlier because it could take some time.

Chop the bell pepper (I'm using several baby peppers here) into bite-sized chunks. If you want to add onion (we're not huge fans in this house) then chop that up too.

Heat a medium-sized pan to high heat, then add coconut oil (or any type) once it's hot. Once the oil is sufficiently heated, try tossing in a pepper or two. If it sizzles, it's ready, so throw all the veg in!

Now DON'T STIR IT YET. However, you'll want to watch the peppers carefully to make sure they don't burn. After a minute or two, flip one over. If it has a nice sear mark on it, then start to stir the peppers and onions around and turn down the heat to medium or low.

Once the peppers are cooking on low heat, you can go ahead and cover the pan and start the next step.


Make the Sweet 'n Sour Sauce

While the peppers are cooking, open up your can of pineapple. Drain the pineapple juice (but not the pineapples) into a small mixing bowl. Then add in the Ketchup and Soy Sauce. Whisk to combine.


Add the Pineapple

Check on the peppers! They should be getting pretty soft and yummy. Once they are, you can go ahead and add the pineapple we saved from earlier. Continue to cook on low heat.

Adding the Chicken

When the chicken is ready, take it out of the oven and move to a chopping board. Cut it into bite sized chunks.

Toss the chicken in the pot. Now we are ready to turn up the heat to medium or high. Go ahead and stir the pot, but be careful not to stir too vigorously or you could knock all the breading off of the chicken (another plus side of chicken nuggets--they don't lose their breading so easily).

When the pan is heated again, add in your sauce, stirring a bit more vigorously to combine the sauce. It should thicken because of the breading in the chicken. If it's too thin, you can add a teaspoon of cornstarch or tapioca starch, according to package directions.



Ta-Da!

That's it! Pretty easy, huh? Serve with warm rice and be ready to wow your family!



Sweet and Sour Chicken

By Rach's Recipes

Ingredients:

  • 10-15 Chicken Nuggets OR 2-3 Chicken Patties OR 2-3 Chicken Strips
  • 1/2 Onion (optional)
  • 1 Bell Pepper (or several baby peppers)
  • 1 16oz can of diced Pineapple (or you can sub pineapple juice with a small can of mandarin oranges)
  • 2 Tbsps Soy Sauce
  • 2 Tbsps Ketchup
  • Cornstarch/Tapioca Starch (Optional)
  • 1/2 Tsp Salt (or to taste)

Instructions:

  1. Place breaded chicken on a pan and bake according to package directions. Be sure to start early if needed--the rest of the recipe only takes about 10-20 minutes!

  2. Chop onions and peppers into bite sized pieces.

  3. Heat oil in a large skillet. When oil is sufficiently hot, throw onions and peppers in. Resist the urge to stir veggies until they've had a chance to sear (1-2 minutes) then stir and add salt and pepper, to taste.

  4. Turn the heat to low, then cover and let the pepeprs and onions cook.

  5. Open the can of pineapple and drain the juice into a bowl. Add the ketchup and soysauce to the bowl, and whisk to combine.

  6. Add the canned/drained pineapple to the peppers and onions

  7. Remove Chicken from the oven and cut into bite sized pieces.

  8. Add the chicken to the pot, and stir carefully. Don't overstir or the breading will come loose.

  9. Turn the heat up to high. Once the whole pot is hot, add the sauce and stir a bit more vigorously. If the sauce doesn't thicken as much as you'd like, add some cornstarch.
